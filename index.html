Why Latitud's fund structure is built to outperform

Latitud enters at pre-seed valuations in Latin America — 3–10x lower than comparable US funds — while its portfolio companies compete for the same global exit opportunities. Combined with a 35-company portfolio that provides more shots on goal, the result is a fund structure that requires dramatically fewer winners to deliver outsized returns. The analyses below compare Latitud to three typical fund archetypes using simple arithmetic — no probability models, just the math of entry pricing, ownership, and fund size.

How many big exits to return the fund?

The simplest question in venture: how many portfolio companies need to produce a big exit for LPs to get their money back — and then some? Latitud's combination of low entry pricing and a smaller fund means it needs far fewer winners.

2

Same portfolio outcomes, different fund returns

Imagine every fund's portfolio produced the exact same total exit enterprise value. Because of differences in fund size and ownership, the MOIC each fund delivers to LPs would be dramatically different. Latitud's smaller fund and efficient entry pricing means each dollar of exit value translates to a higher return.

3

What hit rate does each fund need?

By dividing the number of required big exits by the total portfolio size, we get a required "hit rate" — the percentage of companies that must produce a major outcome. This is a clean, model-free measure of how forgiving each fund's structure is.
